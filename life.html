A life fully lived

A life fully lived

Dr. Krishnaswamy Kasturirangan passes away on 25 April 2025, at the age of 84, in Bengaluru. He leaves behind every satellite that still orbits, every school reformed, every forest protected. Prime Minister Modi wrote: "ISRO attained a new identity under his leadership." The curiosity never stopped.

Education must not only inform, but inspire the capacity to think, question, and create.
2017
Chairs NEP drafting committee

Chairs NEP drafting committee

Appointed to lead the committee tasked with drafting India's National Education Policy. He brings to the task the same integrative philosophy he applied to space science, weaving together access, equity, multidisciplinarity, cultural roots, and the needs of a knowledge economy.

2004
Director, NIAS Bengaluru

Director, NIAS Bengaluru

Appointed Director of the National Institute of Advanced Studies in Bengaluru, a unique institution at the intersection of science, humanities, and public policy. He served until 2009, nurturing the kind of interdisciplinary inquiry that defined his own intellectual life.

1999
Chandrayaan-1 proposed

Chandrayaan-1 proposed

Puts forward the proposal for India's first lunar exploration mission, Chandrayaan-1, articulating a vision for India to enter the planetary era. The mission eventually launched in 2008, successfully identifies water molecules on the Moon's surface and fundamentally reframes scientific understanding of lunar geology.

1994
Chairman of ISRO

Chairman of ISRO

Appointed fifth Chairman of ISRO, also serving as Secretary to the Government of India (Department of Space) and Chairman of the Space Commission. His nine-year tenure, the longest in ISRO's history, transforms India into a credible, self-reliant space-faring nation and puts it among the world's six major space powers.

1982
Padma Shri awarded

Padma Shri awarded

Receives the Padma Shri, the fourth-highest civilian honour in India, recognising early contributions to space science. Around this period he also oversees development of the INSAT-2 and IRS-1A/1B satellites at the ISRO Satellite Centre, helping India build its first generation of operational remote sensing capability.
